Overview

The Gong-A-Long is a unique race, taking in 14km of the stunning Illawarra coastline, under the backdrop of the iconic escarpment. Established in 2020, following the cancellation of other major events, the Gong-A-Long has become a mainstay on the NSW race calendar, often attracting three to four runners from multiple countries (Australia and England).

Following a short hiatus, the Gong-A-Long is back and set for its biggest year yet.

Start

Location

The race will start at the east end of Henley Road, Thirroul (towards the northern point of Thirroul Beach).

Time

  • Sunday, 12th October 2025
  • 9am briefing.
  • 9:15am race start.

Course

The Gong-A-Long takes you on a point-to-point journey along the Illawarra’s spectacular coastline, starting in Thirroul and finishing near North Wollongong. At just over 14 kilometres, the course is approachable in distance but rich in scenery, linking together beaches, headlands, and foreshore paths under the constant presence of the escarpment.

Description

From the start line, runners will head back up Henley Road, before turning south onto Lawrence Hargrave Drive for roughly 300m. Turning left onto The Esplanade, runners will find themselves on the coastal path that they will follow for the majority of the run.

There is a toilet block at Thirroul Beach, just under a kilometre into the run. Heading south from Thirroul Beach, runners will follow the road up the first of two small hills. The course is generally straightforward from this point. Stick to the path, head south. If you need to make a choice between the mountains and the ocean, stick to the ocean.

12km in, the course will pass the Fairy Meadow Surf Life Saving Club. From here, follow the gravel path through Puckeys Estate Reserve. When the path hits Fairy Creek, a right turn will take you along the boardwalk to Squires Way. Runners will then head east and follow the grass along the edge of Stuart Park, eventually coming to the finish line at the park’s southernmost point.

Please note that there will be no course markings of any kind.

Transport

Getting there

  • From Central: Take the train to Austinmer Station. You'll need to change trains at Thirroul. The start line is a ten minute walk from the station.
  • From Wollongong: Take the train to Austinmer Station. Walk ten minutes to the start line.
  • By car: Street parking is available on Henley Road and Mary Street.

Getting home

Make sure you hang around for lunch or a drink before you go.

North Wollongong station is a 20 minute walk from the finish line. If you drove to the start line, you can take the train back, or get a lift.
